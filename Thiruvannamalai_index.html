Thiruvannamalai, also known as Arunachala, is a town in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, famous for the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Annamalaiyar Temple. It is one of the holiest places in Hinduism and attracts pilgrims and tourists alike. The town is situated at the foothills of the Annamalai hills and is considered a sacred pilgrimage site. Thiruvannamalai is closely associated with the Annamalaiyar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple complex is known for its grand architecture and spiritual significance. The town is also famous for the deep spiritual practices and meditation associated with the holy Arunachala hill. Pilgrims from various parts of the country visit Thiruvannamalai to participate in festivals, rituals, and to circumambulate the Arunachala hill. The serene atmosphere and the spiritual vibes make Thiruvannamalai a unique destination for seekers of divine wisdom.

Key Features

  • Renowned Spiritual Hub: Thiruvannamalai is a revered spiritual destination attracting devotees and seekers from around the world.
  • Arunachaleswarar Temple: Home to the sacred Arunachaleswarar Temple, one of the Pancha Bhoota Stalas representing the fire element.
  • Deepam Festival: Witness the grand celebration of the Karthigai Deepam festival, a significant event that involves lighting a huge lamp on the Annamalai Hill.
  • Sri Ramana Maharshi Ashram: Visit the ashram of Sri Ramana Maharshi, a spiritual teacher known for his teachings on self-inquiry and meditation.
  • Annmalaiyar Temple Car Festival: Experience the vibrant and lively chariot festival at the Annamalaiyar Temple.
  • Scenic Beauty: Enjoy the picturesque landscapes surrounding the Annamalai hills, providing a serene and tranquil atmosphere.
  • Tiruvannamalai Girivalam: Undertake the sacred Girivalam, a circumambulation around the Annamalai hills considered spiritually significant.
  • Ashrams and Meditation Centers: Thiruvannamalai is home to various ashrams and meditation centers, making it a hub for spiritual seekers.
